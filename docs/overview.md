# Pony Pastures

A cozy pixel-art horse farm for your pocket.

## The Feeling

You open your phone and step into a tiny world of wildflower meadows, weathered fence posts, and the soft nicker of a horse you've been earning the trust of. You feed her a lavender sprig. She stays a little longer this time. Tomorrow, maybe she'll let you name her.

Pony Pastures isn't a game you win. It's a place you tend.

## The Vision

A stripped-down Stardew Valley rebuilt around a single relationship: you and the horses. No combat, no skill trees, no sprawling systems. Just dirt, seeds, patience, and the slow magic of a wild animal deciding to trust you.

What you grow determines who shows up. Every horse has a flower it loves. You don't know which one. You experiment, observe, and figure it out. Then you grow fields of it. Then you wait. Then one morning, there she is at the fence.

## Core Loop

```
Plant → Water → Harvest → Sell or Feed
  ↑                              |
  |    ┌─────────────────────────┘
  |    ↓
  |  Wild horses visit → Discover favorites → Build trust
  |                                              |
  └──── Tamed horses boost your farm ←───────────┘
```

## The Layers

**Fast loop (seconds):** Plant, water, harvest, sell. Quick taps, coins going up. This is what your hands are doing.

**Medium loop (minutes):** A horse appears at the fence. You feed it a flower. Did it like it? Trust nudges up. It wanders off. A few minutes later, another visit.

**Slow loop (sessions):** A horse is finally tamed. It slots into your stable and its passive bonus kicks in. Everything compounds.

**Infinity loop (forever):** Tamed horses aren't done. Keep feeding them and their bonus levels up. The farm visually transforms as bonuses stack. Cookie-clicker warmth — you open the app and your passive farm is rolling.

## Trust Mechanic

You don't catch horses. You earn them. A wild horse appears because you grew something it loves. Feed it the right flower and trust inches up — but only once per visit. Rush it and the horse spooks.

Common horses take a handful of feedings. The rarest take many. This is the heart of the game: a relationship on the horse's terms.

## Discovery

You start knowing nothing. A mystery horse shows up. You try a daisy — it turns away. You try lavender — it stays. You write it down in the Horse Whisper Journal. Now you know.

The Journal is a subtle log — it quietly tracks what you've tried, what worked, what didn't. No heavy UI, just a reference when you need it.

## The World

One eternal warm world. Golden meadows by day. Cozy dark blue washed in campfire glow by night. The palette shifts gently between the two. Flowers bob. Horses breathe. Everything feels alive but unhurried.

8 flowers. 8 horses. 12 garden plots. The system is deliberately small so every element matters.

## Horse Perks (Passive Stacking)

Each tamed horse adds a passive bonus that levels up the more you feed it:

- **Chestnut Mare** — Auto-plows plots
- **Appaloosa** — Flowers sell for more
- **Golden Palomino** — Chance to double harvests
- **Paint Horse** — Flowers grow faster
- **Black Stallion** — Bonus coins at night
- **Frost Pony** — Rare seed drops
- **Shadow Runner** — Reveals hidden bonuses
- **Starlight Unicorn** — Multiplies everything

Each horse you tame makes the next easier to chase. The farm snowballs, but gently.

## Design Principles

- **No timers, no paywalls, no notifications.** Respects your time. Never punishes you for putting it down.
- **Discovery over instruction.** The game doesn't tell you what each horse wants. You figure it out.
- **Slow warmth over fast action.** Every interaction is gentle. The joy is watching something grow.
- **Small world, deep roots.** Deliberately constrained so every element interconnects.

## The Promise

A tiny world where the only thing that matters is growing something beautiful and being patient with something wild.
